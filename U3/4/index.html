All Mexican families are different and they can vary in the number of

members they have. However, family is still the number one priority among

many modern Mexicans. There are families which include a father, a mother

and one child. There are others which can have two or more children. There

are single-parent families. This means that there is only one parent to look

after children. There are families called extended families, and this includes

grandparents and sometimes several children, siblings with their spouses, too. All of them living together in the same house. What kind of family do you have? A website suggests that while some Mexican families are still traditional, many others have opted to copy family trends similar to those in the U.S. and Europe. Families are changing from extended to nuclear families. This means that there are two parents and their children. Families will continue to change, so we ’ll have to wait and see what the future holds.
